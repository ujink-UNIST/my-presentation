Beam and Solid Models Predict Different Scaling

Beam elements idealize each strut as a 1D member. Solid elements resolve finite strut thickness, joint volume, and local 3D stress states.

  • The two models produce topology-dependent stiffness scaling, so one global correction factor cannot align them.
  • The shaded region marks VF ≥ 1, where beam volume fraction over-counts overlapping joint volumes.
  • The exponent plot fits only VF < 1 and compares the power law Eeff/Es = C·VFn.
  • Beam models support rapid screening; solid FEM drives the final evaluator and modal dataset.

Conclusion: keep solid FEM for high-fidelity data, then reduce the number of runs with active learning.
