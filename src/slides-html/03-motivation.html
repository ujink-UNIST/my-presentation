Low-Frequency Vibration vs. Lightweight Design

Problem

Low-frequency vibration

  • Road and vehicle excitation is transmitted to the seat frame.
  • Low-frequency structural resonance can degrade ride comfort.
  • Multiple global modes may contribute to the vibration response.

Conventional solution

Additional dampers

  • Multi-axis vibration dampers can suppress structural vibration.
  • However, they increase mass and structural complexity.
  • This conflicts with lightweight seat-frame design.

Design conflict

Suppression ⟷ lightweighting

Can the seat frame itself be designed to avoid low-frequency resonance without adding extra damping mass?
