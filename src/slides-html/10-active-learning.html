Implemented basis and planned loop

  • Implemented basis: periodic graph workflow, implicit geometry, meshing, PBC homogenization, MAPDL input generation, and result extraction.
  • Design space can extend by removing a strut, perturbing strut position, or changing local geometry parameters.
  • Planned surrogate: graph-based model with node/edge features, uncertainty estimation, and acquisition-based FEM selection.
  • Stop when target accuracy is reached or FEM budget is exhausted.

Cost ranges to mention verbally: meshing usually takes about 30 seconds to 2 minutes, but volume meshing can exceed 2 hours when CUDA acceleration is disabled. Each strain solve is within about 3 minutes, while modal analysis is about 30 minutes. The eight current lattice graphs are a benchmark topology set: binary strut on/off patterns are symmetry-replicated into the cell, similar to cases used in several lattice papers. The workflow is more flexible because it can also remove individual struts, perturb strut positions, or modify local geometry. Learning-curve evidence versus random sampling is not shown yet because paired experiments are not sufficient.
